# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Hiring managers, tech leads, and senior developers at frontend-focused companies and startups. Context: a brief tab in a multi-tab review session, often on a laptop, scanning for taste and signal within the first 10 seconds. Secondary: other developers who land via a link or social share.

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio for Niko Pranata, a CS student and aspiring frontend developer targeting a frontend web developer internship. The portfolio is the pitch: it must demonstrate real frontend skill through how it is built and how it looks, not just through what it claims. Success means a recruiter reaches out.

## Brand Personality

Calm. Crafted. Curious.

Quiet confidence: restrained, nothing wasted, the work speaks for itself. Warm enough to feel like a person, not a resume printed in pixels.

## Anti-references

- Generic dev portfolio: dark background, neon green or blue accent, "Hello World, I'm a developer" hero text, identical project card grids.
- Anything that leads with personality over craft (flashy intros, cursor effects as the main event).
- SaaS landing page aesthetics: gradient text, glassmorphic cards, floating blobs, metric-hero sections.

## Design Principles

1. **Practice what you preach.** The portfolio itself is a frontend project. Code quality, animation craft, and responsive behavior are part of the portfolio, not decoration on it.
2. **Type as structure.** Editorial typography carries hierarchy. Don't reach for decorative elements to create visual interest; let scale, weight, and spacing do the work.
3. **Restraint is a decision.** Every empty space is chosen, not defaulted to. Absence is a design choice, not a gap to fill.
4. **Show, don't list.** Project presentation is experiential. Let the visitor feel the work, not just read its description.
5. **Work earns the spotlight.** The design frames the work without competing with it. If the portfolio itself is more memorable than the projects, something is wrong.
6. **Personality.** calm, crafted, curious — restraint over maximalism. 
  Every element earns its place.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance. Dark-first: dark theme is the designed and intentional state; light mode is a supported alternate. Respect prefers-reduced-motion for all animations.
